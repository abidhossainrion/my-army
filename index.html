History of BMA



After the independence of Bangladesh, the Father of the Nation Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman envisioned the necessity to establish a military training institution to train and build up a professional, dedicated and patriotic army officers who will safeguard the sovereignty, integrity and freedom of our nation. To materialize his noble vision Bangladesh Military Academy (BMA) was established and inaugurated at Cumilla Cantonment in 1974. The academy commenced its journey with 1st BMA Short Course. Col Khondakar Nazmul Huda, BB, was appointed the first Commandant (ex officio) of the Academy. BMA was shifted to its present location at Bhatiary, Chattogram in January 1976. In 1979, Bangladesh Military Academy was awarded with the prestigious ‘National Standard’ as recognition of its significant contribution to the nation. The training curricula of the officer cadets are designed following the framework of an Infantry Battalion to ensure a comprehensive and practical oriented military training.

Chief of Army Staff's Note

Bangladesh Military Academy (BMA), our premier military training institution, is a perennial source of our pride and dignity. The unique academy came into being to train and build up a professional, devoted, patriotic and ever spirited officers for our beloved nation. BMA has come a long way and has been relentlessly pursuing in its onward progression with glorious success and prosperity. We are fully focused on further harnessing BMA's world class training facilities to exhibit an aura of efficiency in the changing scenario of the world. I firmly believe that BMA shall remain ever committed to uphold its glorious traditions as it is one of the premier military training academies in the world.
